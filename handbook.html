What is Matrix?

Matrix is your personal task manager built for Maruti Suzuki. It uses the Eisenhower Priority Method — a simple but powerful system to decide which tasks need your attention right now and which ones can wait, be delegated, or dropped entirely. Think of it as a to-do list with a brain.

💡 The core idea: Every task you have should live in exactly one of four boxes based on two questions — Is it urgent? Is it important?
The Four Quadrants
Q1 — DO FIRST (Red)
Urgent & Important
These are on-fire tasks — deadlines, crises, emergencies. Work on these immediately. Example: Customer complaint that must be resolved today.
Q2 — SCHEDULE (Navy)
Not Urgent but Important
This is where growth happens. Plan these for your calendar. Example: Writing your quarterly review, learning a new skill.
Q3 — DELEGATE (Green)
Urgent but Not Important
These feel urgent but don't need you specifically. Hand them off. Example: Routine report filing, meeting scheduling.
Q4 — ELIMINATE (Amber)
Not Urgent & Not Important
Time-wasters. Minimize or remove these. Example: Unnecessary browsing, trivial admin tasks.
⚠️ Tip: Most people spend too much time in Q1 & Q3. The goal is to move more work to Q2 — that's where you do your best work calmly.

Managing Tasks

Adding a Task

  • 1Click the + button in the quadrant where the task belongs.
  • 2Type your task description in the text box that appears.
  • 3Optionally pick a Category (tag), a Due Date, and an Assignee (person).
  • 4Press Enter or click the blue tick (✓) to save.
💡 Write tasks as actions: "Call supplier" not "Supplier".

Completing a Task

  • 1Click the circle checkbox on the left of any task card.
  • 2The task text will get a strikethrough — it's marked done!
  • 3Click the circle again to un-complete it.

Editing a Task

  • 1Click anywhere on a task card (not the checkbox) to open it.
  • 2An edit form opens — change the text, quadrant, category, due date, or assignee.
  • 3Click Save to apply changes.

Deleting a Task

  • 1Open the task by clicking it.
  • 2Click the red Delete button in the edit modal.
  • 3Confirm deletion in the pop-up that appears. This cannot be undone.

Clearing Completed Tasks

  • 1In the settings panel (⚙), find Clear Done Tasks.
  • 2Choose to clear done tasks from one quadrant or all quadrants at once.
⚠️ Remember: Deleting a task is permanent. If you just finished something, tick the checkbox instead of deleting it — completed tasks count in your Stats.

Recurring Tasks

When editing a task, you can set a Recurrence (Daily, Weekly, Monthly). When you tick a recurring task as done, it automatically reappears the next day/week/month so you never forget regular duties.

Moving Tasks & Categories

Moving a Task Between Quadrants

Priorities change. You can re-classify a task two ways:

Method 1 — Drag & Drop

  • 1Click and hold a task card.
  • 2Drag it over the quadrant you want to move it to (it will highlight).
  • 3Release to drop. The task moves instantly.

Method 2 — Edit Modal

  • 1Click the task card to open the edit form.
  • 2Change the Quadrant dropdown.
  • 3Click Save.
📊 Every move is logged automatically. You can see your quadrant movement history in the Stats dashboard.

Categories

Categories are coloured labels (like "Finance", "HR", "Operations") that help you group and filter tasks.

Creating a Category

  • 1Click the ⚙ Settings icon in the top-right.
  • 2Go to the Categories section.
  • 3Type a name and pick a colour, then click Add.

Using a Category

  • 1When adding or editing a task, select a category from the dropdown.
  • 2A coloured tag appears on the task card.

Filtering by Category

  • 1Click a category button above the matrix grid to show only tasks with that category.
  • 2Click All to remove the filter.

Due Dates & Reminders

Set a Due Date
Pick a date when adding or editing any task. Tasks with dates show the date on their card.
Overdue Warning
If a task is past its due date, the date shows in red automatically.
Email Reminders
Click 🔔 to configure a daily digest email of your pending tasks.

Delegation & People

The Delegate quadrant (Q3) is where tasks live that someone else should handle. Matrix lets you assign any task to a specific person — so you always know who owns what.

Setting Up People

Before you can assign tasks, create the people you work with.

  • 1Click ⚙ Settings in the top-right.
  • 2Click Manage People.
  • 3Click + Add Person, type their name, and save.
  • 4Each person gets a unique colour automatically.
👤 Add yourself as a person and assign tasks to Self to track your own workload separately from delegated work.

Assigning a Task

  • 1Open any task by clicking it.
  • 2In the edit form, find the Assignee field.
  • 3Select a person from the list.
  • 4Save the task — the person's name now appears on the task card.

Best Practices & Troubleshooting

Daily Routine (5 minutes)

WhenAction
MorningCheck Q1 — handle anything urgent first
During dayAdd tasks as they come; put them in the right quadrant
End of dayTick completed tasks; move anything that changed priority
WeeklyReview Q2, assign due dates, check Stats dashboard, export Excel

Golden Rules

  • ✓Never leave Q3 empty of owners. Every delegated task needs a person.
  • ✓Keep Q1 small. If it keeps growing, you need more Q2 planning.
  • ✓Zero overdue = success. Act on due dates before they pass.
  • ✓One task, one action. Write tasks as single clear actions.
