The Eisenhower Matrix Explained

The Eisenhower Matrix — named after President Dwight D. Eisenhower — is a simple framework for deciding what to work on. Every task is judged on two dimensions: urgency (does it need attention today?) and importance (does it move meaningful goals forward?). The four combinations create four quadrants, each with a clear action strategy.

💡 The core idea: Every task belongs in exactly one quadrant. Two questions decide which — Is it urgent? Is it important? Be honest with yourself.
Q1 — DO FIRST · Urgent & Important
Act on these immediately — they cannot wait.
These are crises, hard deadlines, and emergencies that only you can handle. Keep this quadrant as small as possible; a large Q1 means too many things have been left until they became urgent. Examples: Critical customer complaint that must be resolved today, a presentation due in two hours, a production line stoppage.
Q2 — SCHEDULE · Not Urgent but Important
Plan time for these — this is where your best work lives.
Strategic, growth-oriented tasks that have no immediate deadline but are essential to long-term success. If you never protect time for Q2, everything eventually explodes into Q1. Examples: Preparing a quarterly review, building a new process, developing your skills, relationship-building with key stakeholders.
Q3 — DELEGATE · Urgent but Not Important
These feel urgent but do not need you specifically — hand them off.
Tasks that must be done now but do not require your particular expertise or authority. These are prime delegation candidates. Always assign a named person so accountability is clear. Examples: Routine report distribution, meeting room booking, data entry, standard approvals.
Q4 — ELIMINATE · Not Urgent & Not Important
Minimise or remove these entirely.
Activities that consume time without producing value. Ask yourself honestly whether this task needs to exist at all. Examples: Unnecessary email chains, redundant meetings, excessive social browsing during work hours, trivial admin.
⚠️ Common mistake: Most people live in Q1 and Q3 and neglect Q2. The goal is to shift more work into Q2 — that is where you do your best, most focused work calmly and proactively.

Managing Tasks

Adding a Task

  • 1Find the quadrant where the task belongs (Q1–Q4).
  • 2Click the inline text field at the bottom of that quadrant and type your task.
  • 3Press Enter to save immediately. The task appears in the list.
💡 Write tasks as clear actions: "Call supplier about delivery" not "Supplier".

Editing a Task

  • 1Click the pencil icon on any task card to open the edit form.
  • 2Change the text, quadrant, category, or assignee as needed.
  • 3Click Save to apply changes.

Deleting a Task

  • 1Click the X icon on the task card.
  • 2The task is permanently removed. This cannot be undone.

Marking a Task Done

  • 1Click the checkbox on the left of any task card.
  • 2The task immediately shows at 50% opacity (faded) to indicate it is complete.
  • 3Click the checkbox again to un-complete it.

Q1 Priority Colour Gradient

Tasks in Q1 (Do First) are automatically colour-coded by their position in the list to help you see at a glance which task is the most urgent.

■ Top task — most urgent (red)
■ Second task
■ Third task
■ Further down
■ Lowest urgency in Q1 (blue)
🌟 The gradient runs red→blue from top to bottom. Reorder Q1 tasks by dragging them up or down to control the urgency signal.

Drag and Drop Between Quadrants

On desktop you can drag any task card from one quadrant to another.

  • 1Click and hold a task card to pick it up.
  • 2Drag it over the target quadrant — the panel highlights to show it will accept the drop.
  • 3Release to move the task. It lands in the new quadrant instantly.
📱 Mobile: Drag and drop is not available on touchscreens. Open the pencil edit form and change the Quadrant dropdown instead.

Categories, Search & Archive

Categories

Categories are coloured tags — like "Work", "Personal", "Finance" — that appear as a small badge on each task card. They let you group tasks visually and understand where your time is going.

Managing Categories

  • 1Click the ⚙ Gear icon in the top bar to open Settings.
  • 2Select Manage Categories.
  • 3Type a name, pick a colour, and click Add. Repeat for each category you need.
  • 4Delete a category by clicking the X next to its name. Tasks with that category lose the badge.

Applying a Category to a Task

  • 1Click the pencil icon on a task card to open the edit form.
  • 2Choose a category from the Category dropdown.
  • 3Save — a coloured badge now appears on the task card.
Work Personal Urgent Finance HR

Search

The search bar in the top bar filters tasks in real time across all four quadrants simultaneously.

  • 1Click the search bar at the top of the page.
  • 2Start typing any keyword — tasks that do not match are hidden immediately.
  • 3Clear the search box to show all tasks again.
🔍 Search works across all quadrants at once. You do not need to check each quadrant separately to find a task.

Archive

Tasks that have been marked done for 7 or more days are automatically moved to the Archive. This keeps your quadrants clean without permanently deleting completed work.

Delegation & People

The Delegate quadrant (Q3) exists specifically for tasks that should be handled by someone else. Matrix lets you build a personal list of people and assign Q3 tasks to them — so accountability is always visible on the task card.

Adding People

People are managed through the dedicated people icon in the top bar — not the gear/settings icon.

  • 1Click the 👥 People icon (two figures) in the top bar.
  • 2Click + Add Person, type their name, and save.
  • 3Each person is saved to your list and is available in the Assignee dropdown when editing any task.
  • 4To remove someone, click the X next to their name in the People panel.
👥 Add your whole team here. Even if someone only receives one task, having them in the list makes tracking easy and prevents unowned Q3 tasks.

Assigning a Q3 Task

  • 1Click the pencil icon on any task card (especially Q3 tasks).
  • 2In the edit form, find the Assignee field and select a person.
  • 3Click Save — the person's name now appears as a chip on the task card.

You can assign tasks in any quadrant, but it is most meaningful in Q3 where delegation is the primary purpose.

The Assignee Chip

Once a person is assigned, their name appears as a small chip on the task card — visible at a glance without opening the task.

Email Reminders

Matrix can send you automatic email reminders about your pending tasks on a schedule you choose. Reminders are powered by Microsoft Power Automate running in the background — you do not need to keep the app open for them to fire. Once configured and saved, they run independently on schedule.

Setting Up Reminders

  • 1Click the 🔔 Bell icon in the top bar to open Reminder Settings.
  • 2Toggle Email Reminders to ON.
  • 3Choose your preferred frequency from the list (see table).
  • 4Click Save. The modal will show "Next reminder: date/time" to confirm it is active.
🔔 After saving, close the app entirely. Reminders will still arrive in your Outlook inbox on schedule because they run in Power Automate, not in the browser.

Turning Reminders Off

  • 1Click the 🔔 Bell icon to open Reminder Settings.
  • 2Toggle Email Reminders to OFF.
  • 3Click Save. No further reminder emails will be sent.
⚠️ If you do not click Save, your changes will not take effect — the previous schedule remains active.

Mobile Guide

Matrix is fully optimised for smartphones and tablets. The mobile layout replaces the 2×2 desktop grid with a bottom tab bar — one tab per quadrant plus a dedicated Archive tab. You work on one quadrant at a time by switching tabs.

Bottom Tab Bar

Five tabs appear at the bottom of the screen on mobile:

TabQuadrant
Do FirstQ1 — Urgent & Important
ScheduleQ2 — Not Urgent & Important
DelegateQ3 — Urgent & Not Important
EliminateQ4 — Not Urgent & Not Important
ArchiveArchived completed tasks (5th tab — only shown when archived tasks exist)
📲 Tap any tab to switch quadrants. Swipe left/right on the task list to navigate between quadrants quickly without using the tabs.

Adding Tasks on Mobile

  • 1Tap the tab for the quadrant where the task belongs.
  • 2Tap the inline text field at the bottom of the list.
  • 3Type the task and tap Enter on your keyboard.

Moving Tasks on Mobile

Drag and drop is not available on touch screens. To move a task to a different quadrant, use the edit form:

  • 1Tap the pencil icon on the task card to open the edit form.
  • 2Tap the Quadrant dropdown and select the destination quadrant.
  • 3Tap Save. The task disappears from the current tab and moves to the new one.

Archive Tab on Mobile

  • 1Tap the Archive tab (5th tab) to see all auto-archived tasks.
  • 2Tap the undo (↩) button on a task to restore it to its original quadrant.
  • 3Tap the X button to permanently delete an archived task.
💾 The Archive tab only appears when there are tasks that have been done for 7+ days. If you don't see it, no tasks have been archived yet.

Best Practices & Troubleshooting

Daily Routine (5 minutes)

WhenAction
MorningOpen Q1 — handle the top red task first before anything else
During dayAdd new tasks as they arise; classify into the correct quadrant immediately
After each taskTick the checkbox to mark done — keeps completion rate accurate
End of dayReview Q1 and Q3; reassign anything that changed priority
Weekly (Friday)Review Q2 dates, check Stats charts, export Excel for records

Golden Rules

  • ✓Keep Q1 small. If it keeps growing, invest more time in Q2 planning. Crises shrink when you plan ahead.
  • ✓Every Q3 task needs an owner. An unassigned Q3 task is just an urgent task with no plan.
  • ✓Use categories consistently. They make the Stats charts meaningful and help you see workload by area.
  • ✓Tick done, don't delete. Completed tasks count in your Stats. Deleting removes them from history.
  • ✓Set reminder frequency honestly. 30-minute reminders work well for active days. Weekly for review-style users.
